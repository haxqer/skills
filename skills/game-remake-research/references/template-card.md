# Card Template

Use this template when the game's identity depends on a precise rules engine, timing windows, resource curve management, card-pool ecology, and matchup health.

Read `metrics-card.md` when you need measurable baselines for turn pace, curve shape, and meta concentration.

## What Makes The Research Different

- Precision matters more than vibes.
- Timing windows and visibility rules must be explicit.
- Collection and onboarding quality can be as important as match rules.
- Archetype ecology matters more than isolated card power.

## Focus By Role

### Product Manager

- Define whether the player promise is competitive mastery, collection, puzzle solving, drafting, or spectacle.
- Capture onboarding deck quality and long-term card-pool churn.

### Professional Game Designer

- Map the match loop, collection loop, release loop, and meta loop.
- Separate fantasy from rules rigor; both must survive the remake.

### Balance Designer

- Document resource curve targets, draw smoothing, mulligan, card-rate expectations, and anti-snowball controls.
- Capture archetype health, matchup spread, and tech-answer surfaces.

### Gameplay Designer

- Specify phases, priority, stack or queue behavior, target legality, hidden information, and resolution order.
- Document board-state complexity and turn-time pressure.

### Art / Animation / Audio

- Capture readability of board state, targeting, card rarity, and result feedback under high information density.

### Client Architect / Lead Engineer

- Model the rules engine explicitly: zones, events, triggers, continuous effects, resolution order, and log visibility.
- Identify tool needs for card authoring, keyword reuse, and balance validation.

## Must Capture

- Turn-state structure and timing windows.
- Resource curve and anti-snowball mechanisms.
- Card ecology: types, keywords, copy limits, archetypes.
- Collection, crafting, draft, or rotation loop.
- Board-state readability and rules visibility.

## Additional Deliverables

- Turn-state diagram.
- Keyword glossary and timing reference.
- Resource-curve target table.
- Archetype and matchup matrix.
- Card-pool release or rotation plan.

## Common Remake Traps

- Copying card fantasies without rebuilding the rules engine.
- Leaving timing ambiguous and creating inconsistent interactions.
- Ignoring onboarding deck quality or collection friction.
- Balancing individual cards without understanding archetype ecology.
